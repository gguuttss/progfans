Ryan "Quicksave" Romano is an eccentric adventurer with a strange power: he can create a save-point in time and redo his life whenever he dies. Arriving in New Rome, the glitzy capital of sin of a rebuilding Europe, he finds the city torn between mega-corporations, sponsored heroes, superpowered criminals, and true monsters. It's a time of chaos, where potions can grant the power to rule the world and dangers lurk everywhere. 

Ryan only sees different routes; and from Hero to Villain, he has to try them all. Only then will he achieve his perfect ending... no matter how many loops it takes.

The Perfect Run updates on Tuesday and Saturday. Cover by Vitaly S. Alexius.

Not very good, but very long.

Reviewed at: 130: Farewell, New Rome (Ending)
by Unprnncibal Sunday, August 25, 2024 2:32:27 AM

I suspect that this story is rated so highly because most people read a chapter or two, realize that it is poorly written, and do not rate it; only people who love it will read enough to rate it. As other reviews have stated, if you do not like it after the first few chapters, don't press on, it is all like that, and often rather worse. But since many people /do/ love it, it's worth reading the first few chapters and seeing what you think. 

Quick notes: fair world-building, very complex story. Pretty good superhero setting -- lots of interesting powers, often used fairly cleverly, although lots of dues ex machina as well. The author's first language is not English, and it shows;  the editing deteriorates somewhat as the story progresses. The characters are exactly what they are; parodies of shallow movie characters; this does not improve. The characters are pretty much all anti-heroes to some extent, but it is unclear if the author knows this, or thinks that causal sadism is funny.

Overall, fast and fun, if you aren't too picky about your fun. The best thing about it is that it manages to keep its tone throughout, which is great if you like it. The second best is the complexity, which is exactly at the level you would hope for in a a time-loop story.

Fantastic Time Loop Story

Reviewed at: 130: Farewell, New Rome (Ending)
by J2theS0 Thursday, May 9, 2024 7:29:15 PM

A Very good Time Loop Series.

This story pretty much has it all.  The MC is witty, courageous, and most importantly hilarious. He sometimes meanders about in moral ambiguity, but in the "Perfect Loop" he always helps the most people he possibly can, even at the detriment of his own happiness.  Though his powers are completely different his attitude and jokes remind me a bit of Ryan Reynold's Deadpool character.

The Story is well thought out and explained well, with flashbacks providing the history and background rather than someone just telling us (which is nice). The supporting characters grow on you and while you sometimes hate they get reset so often, it helps you immerse yourself into the story from the MC's perspective.  The story is also a complete story, with a beginning, a clear plotline (although it morphs a bit as definitions of his perfect loop change), and a well done conclusion. It isn't your typical web novel that often meanders for years without much direction. 

If you are reading this review, I'd recommend reading this novel and pretty much all other novels written by Void Herald. This is his third that I have read and have been pleased with them all.
