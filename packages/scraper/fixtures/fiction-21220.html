Original COMPLETED Time Loop Adventure Fantasy Mystery Magic

Zorian is a teenage mage of humble birth and slightly above-average skill, attending his third year of education at Cyoria's magical academy. He is a driven and irritable young man, consumed by a desire to ensure his own future and free himself of the influence of his family, whom he resents for favoring his brothers over him. Consequently, he has no time for pointless distractions or paying attention to other people's problems.

As it happens, time is something he is about to get plenty of. On the eve of the Cyoria's annual summer festival, he is killed and brought back to the beginning of the month, just before he was about to take a train to Cyoria. Suddenly trapped in a time loop with no clear end or exit, Zorian will have to look both within and without to unravel the mystery before him. And he does have to unravel it, for the time loop hadn't been made for his sake and dangers lurk everywhere...

Repetition is the mother of learning, but Zorian will have to first make sure he survives to try again - in a world of magic, even a time traveler isn't safe from those who wish him ill.

Mother of Learning is amazingly written.

Reviewed at: 21. Wheel of Fortune
by I_have_great_taste Sunday, February 19, 2023 6:25:05 PM

The characters are realistic and unique, the plot twists are unexpected and exciting, and the worldbuilding is detailed and immersive. The story also has a well-developed system of rules that the author follows to maintain consistency and avoid cheating. The author, Domagoj Kurmaic, deserves support on Patreon for the high quality of work produced. The concept of a world trapped in a time loop is executed perfectly, and the main character's growth is shown through his interactions with the world and the people in it. The grammar is excellent, and there is no distracting romantic subplot. Overall, "Mother of Learning" is an amazingly written fantasy novel with lovable characters, a good magic system, and an amazing plot twist. Highly recommended for all fantasy lovers.

The best story on RR

Reviewed at: 14. The Sister Effect
by GiorgosII Sunday, September 1, 2024 3:46:04 PM

The title says it all. This is one of the best stories I have read. I am on my second read right now and the reread vallue is immense. Another sign of a great story.

 

Grammar: Perfect.

Story: I was a little apprehensive at first. I usually don't like stories with time related shenanigans. But after reading the first five chapters I was hooked! The author knew exactly what to reveal in the "first run" so that when the loop started "for us" we had enough to be gripped of something but at the same time not enough as to be able to guess what would happen.

 

Characters: I really loved all the characters. They were all really distinct. Especially our mc's understanding of them as the story progresses is masterful.

 

Style: It is apparent that the author knows how to writte. A story that could become repetive instead added more and more layers upon it with every loop. I personally also like a good romance in my coming of age stories but due the nature of this particullar story and the fact that the loop did not allow for I can completely see how that could not be done. Instead we got a really good friendship (our mc finally got a friend) and one the most perfect brother-sister relationships I have ever read. I am a big brother to a smaller sister and the relationship between Zorian and Kirielle had me laughing, crying and everything in between. Trule spectacular.

 

One of the best stories, after reading it for a second time I may also return for a third...

I don't understand the hype. Where is the hook?

Reviewed at: 4. Stars Fell
by HeavenlyOrder Sunday, January 7, 2024 6:47:03 AM

Maybe I'm not the correct demographic, so I'll premise this with the fact I'm post-college age. 

For all the hype this series got, I trudged thru 3 long chapters and there's still no hook. There's no conflict besides day to day life. The MC is a bit insufferable (and maybe his character arc is that he becomes nice, so I can forgive that), and, I'm sorry to the author, but most paragraphs are long run-on sentences.

Most of the story so far has been "tell" not "show." Long expository dumps of his thoughts or info I wouldn't know because it happened "off screen". 

Im sure this is a great story, based on the reviews, but for someone going in blind, 3 chapters was a lot to read without a payoff or Incentive to keep reading. What's the conflict? What's the story? Nothing has really happened except an annoying teenager has gone off to magic school. 

I may continue to read this story on and off in the future in hopes of it picking up. But for now, I give it a 3.5/5.
